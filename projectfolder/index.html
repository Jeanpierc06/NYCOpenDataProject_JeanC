New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH)


The DOHMH is the city agency responsible for protecting and promoting the health of all New Yorkers. Its work includes disease prevention, environmental health oversight, food and water safety, public health inspections, and enforcing health regulations across the city.

For pools specifically, DOHMH issues permits, conducts routine and complaint-based inspections, and ensures that public and private bathing establishments meet safety and sanitation standards.

Brief Description of the Dataset Used


The dataset used in this website is the NYC Pool Inspections dataset, published by the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH) through the NYC Open Data portal.

It contains detailed records of inspections conducted on indoor and outdoor pools across New York City. Each record represents a single inspection and includes information such as:

Facility name, address, and borough

-Type of permit (indoor or outdoor)

-Inspection date and type (routine, complaint, re-inspection, etc.)

-Number of violations found

-Geographic data like ZIP code and latitude

The dataset currently includes 5,842 rows and 23 columns, updated annually, and serves as a transparent record of pool safety and compliance across the city.

Importance and Relevance of the Dataset

The NYC Pool Inspections dataset, published by the New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH), plays a vital role in promoting public health and safety. It provides transparent access to inspection results for both public and private pools across the five boroughs.
